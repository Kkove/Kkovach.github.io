Online Forums Remediation

Introduction   Usage   Jargon   Hierarchies   Final Thoughts  

Many of the key components of what makes a discourse community beyond just common interests can be seen in how people use online forums for discussion. An informal learning is required for communicating effectively in these chatrooms including jargon, hierarchies, and expected courtesies. It also differs from the common understanding of a discourse community as it takes place entirely digitally. Mary F. Ziegler, Trena Paulus, and Marianne Woodside (2013) looked over many online forums to get an idea of the commonalities between each in order to define how they are discourse communities. The throughline found is that almost every system has rules for posting and those rules get enforced either by website admins or more often than not unpaid moderators. There is an order kept by those in charge to keep language policed and discussions on topic. This can be seen as a sort of enforced discourse community much like religion where in order to be a part of it you must follow a guideline. As can be seen, RetroAchievements embodies all of this.
